The problem for most traders lies in their inability to recognize the moment when the market is coiling before a massive jump. The Squeeze Momentum indicator was developed to solve this exact problem, becoming the gold standard for identifying energy accumulation zones. This tool is based on the understanding that price cannot remain in a tight range for long. The longer the squeeze phase lasts, the more aggressive and sustained the subsequent breakout will be. Understanding this mechanic allows a trader not just to follow the trend, but to enter a position at the very inception of a new impulse, minimizing risks while maximizing potential profit.

## The Synergy of Bollinger and Keltner

The technical foundation of the Squeeze indicator is built on a unique combination of two classic tools: Bollinger Bands and Keltner Channels. Bollinger Bands measure volatility via standard deviation, while Keltner Channels utilize the Average True Range (ATR). A squeeze is confirmed the moment the upper and lower Bollinger Bands move inside the Keltner Channels. Visually, this is displayed on the chart by a change in the color of the dots on the zero line of the indicator. When the dots turn red, it means the market has entered a zone of extremely low volatility — energy is compressed like a spring. As soon as the Bollinger Bands expand back outside the Keltner Channels, the spring releases, and the dots on the indicator change color (usually to gray or green), signaling the start of an explosive move. This is the moment of volatility release, when a trader must be ready to execute a trade.

## Interpreting Histogram Signals

The mere fact of exiting a squeeze is not enough for successful trading; it is critically important to understand the direction and strength of the momentum. To this end, the indicator features an integrated momentum histogram calculated as the price deviation from linear regression. The histogram helps visualize the dominant market force at the moment of the breakout. If, after the squeeze phase, the histogram bars begin to rise above the zero line and turn bright blue or light green, it indicates a powerful bullish impulse. Conversely, if the bars drop below zero and turn bright red, bearish sentiment dominates the market. An important nuance is the change in color shade: when a bright green bar is replaced by a dark green one, it signals a slowdown in momentum, which often serves as a leading indicator to take profit. Thus, the indicator provides a comprehensive picture: the dots on the zero line tell you "when" to enter, while the histogram suggests "where" to move and "how long" to hold the position.

## Entry Strategy and Timeframes

The effectiveness of the Squeeze indicator manifests across various time intervals, but the most reliable signals are formed on higher timeframes, such as H4 or Daily. For short-term traders, using the squeeze on 15-minute or hourly charts also yields results, especially during periods of major macroeconomic data releases. The main strategy involves waiting for the moment when the red dots on the zero line change to a different color. A trade is entered on the first or second bar after the squeeze release, provided the direction is confirmed by the histogram. A stop-loss is traditionally placed behind the opposite boundary of the accumulation range or below the nearest local extreme.
