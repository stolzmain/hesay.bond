## The mechanics of market imbalance
Technically, an FVG is formed in a three-candle structure. An imbalance occurs when the wick of the first candle and the wick of the third candle do not overlap, leaving a void in the body of the second, impulsive candle. This zone indicates that orders on one side of the market were executed so aggressively that the opposite side failed to provide sufficient liquidity. For an analyst, such gaps are priority areas of interest, as the market strives for efficiency and will sooner or later return to cover these voids in search of rebalancing.

## Higher timeframes as a strategic magnet
On daily, weekly, and monthly charts, FVG zones are of colossal importance. They act as powerful magnets for the price. Large hedge funds and banks often rely on these inefficiencies to accumulate positions or take profits. If a bearish FVG forms on the daily chart, it should be treated as a resistance zone where a reversal is highly likely. Ignoring inefficiencies on higher timeframes is a critical mistake, as a liquidity void on a daily chart will always take priority over any local signal on a five-minute chart.

## Tactical precision on lower intervals
When the price enters a higher-order FVG zone, the focus shifts to lower timeframes (M1, M5, M15). Here, the FVG is used for surgical trade entries. Instead of entering at the first touch of a wide zone, a professional looks for a similar imbalance pattern on a one-minute chart after confirming a Change of Character (CHOCH). This allows for a tighter stop-loss and significantly increases the trade expectancy. An FVG on a lower timeframe within a higher-period zone is direct evidence that a big player has begun to defend their interest.

## Fill levels and partial overlap
The market does not always fully cover the Fair Value Gap zone. In trading, it is common to distinguish two key scenarios: touching the FVG boundary and filling up to the 50% level, known as Consequent Encroachment (CE). The 0.5 level within the zone often acts as a strong psychological support or resistance point. If the price closes with a candle body beyond the equator of a bearish imbalance, it often signals seller weakness and the possibility of a full zone fill. Analyzing the price reaction at this level provides deep insight into the true strength of the current trend and the market maker's intentions.

## Multi-structural approach and time-period synergy
The efficiency of the FVG concept is fully realized only when using multi-timeframe analysis. Trading is a game of probabilities, and the highest accuracy occurs at the point of synchronization between several time intervals. For example, when the price corrects to a bullish FVG on a four-hour chart, and a market structure break with the formation of a new imbalance occurs in that same area on the fifteen-minute period. Such a nesting of confirmations minimizes false signals and allows for trading in harmony with the overall context, rather than trying to guess random movements.

## Risk management and goals
FVG is not a magic tool; it is merely a zone of high probability for a reaction.
