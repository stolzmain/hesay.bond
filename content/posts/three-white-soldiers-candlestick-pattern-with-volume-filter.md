## Mechanics of bullish momentum formation

The classic definition of the pattern requires several conditions. Each candle in the sequence should close near the top of its range, featuring a long body and minimal upper wicks. An important nuance is the opening level: in an ideal scenario, each subsequent candle opens within the body of the previous one or at its closing level. This demonstrates an absence of gaps downward and sustained buying pressure. The first candle is the soldier that breaks through local resistance or a consolidation zone following a prolonged decline. The second and third candles confirm the buyers' conviction by pushing the price beyond previous highs. If the candle bodies become smaller by the third position, it may indicate momentum exhaustion, warranting extra caution.

## Psychology of the market shift

Behind the graphical representation of the Three White Soldiers lies a fundamental shift in participant sentiment. After a period of bear dominance, the market reaches a value zone where institutional players begin accumulating positions. The first candle is often the result of short-covering and initial buying by smart money. The second candle draws in breakout traders, while the third symbolizes the capitulation of remaining sellers and the mass entry of retail participants. The psychological strength of this pattern lies in the market closing higher than it opened for three consecutive periods, ignoring bear attempts to regain control. This creates a powerful inertial backdrop that often serves as the foundation for a new long-term uptrend.

## Volume as the primary quality filter

Without analyzing vertical volume, the Three White Soldiers pattern loses a significant portion of its predictive power. In this context, volume acts as the fuel for the move. A professional filter implies following the confirming growth rule: the volume on each of the three candles should be higher than the average value of the previous 10-20 periods. The ideal situation is a progressive increase in volume from the first candle to the third. This indicates that interest in the asset grows as the price rises, which is characteristic of a healthy bull trend. If we see these soldiers on falling or extremely low volume, it signals a hollow move that can be easily absorbed by the market upon even minor selling. Low volume indicates a lack of institutional support, turning the pattern into a potential bull trap.

## Entry and profit-taking mathematics

Trading this setup requires a disciplined approach to risk management. Entry is typically executed at the close of the third candle or on a slight pullback to the equator (midpoint) of the second soldier's body. A conservative stop-loss is placed at the low of the first candle in the pattern, as taking out this level invalidates the bullish scenario entirely. A more aggressive approach involves placing the stop below the low of the second candle.
