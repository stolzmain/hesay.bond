## Identifying Hidden Selling

One of the key advantages of the Net Volume indicator is its ability to identify so-called hidden selling. This is a critical aspect for traders, allowing them to anticipate potential trend reversals before they become obvious to the majority of market participants. Hidden selling manifests as a bearish divergence, where an asset's price continues to form new highs, but the Net Volume indicator fails to confirm this movement, showing a decline or an inability to surpass previous peaks. This situation signals that buying activity is waning and the rising price is not supported by real capital inflow. Large players may begin to gradually distribute their holdings, selling in small increments to avoid triggering panic and a sharp price drop. The Net Volume indicator allows traders to see this activity, which remains invisible to standard volume metrics.

## The Divergence Mechanism

Divergence between price and Net Volume is a classic signal of a possible trend reversal. If an asset is in an uptrend, but Net Volume begins to decline by forming lower highs, it can be a harbinger of an impending reversal or at least a significant correction. This occurs because, without confirmation from volume, the current price action becomes unsustainable, indicating that the initiative is shifting from buyers to sellers, even if this is not yet fully reflected in the price. Analyzing this discrepancy allows traders to make more informed decisions, such as taking profits on long positions or preparing to open short positions. Similarly, a bullish divergence, where the price hits new lows while Net Volume fails to confirm them or starts to rise, can indicate hidden buying and a potential upward reversal.

## Practical Application in Strategies

To effectively utilize the Net Volume indicator, it is essential to integrate it into a comprehensive trading strategy. Net Volume is not a standalone signal for entering or exiting a trade, but it serves as a powerful confirmation filter. Combining Net Volume with other technical analysis tools significantly improves forecast accuracy. For example, it can be used in conjunction with support and resistance levels, candlestick patterns, or oscillators like RSI. If the price breaks a resistance level while Net Volume simultaneously increases, this confirms the strength and validity of the breakout, pointing to a likely continuation of the move. Conversely, if a breakout occurs on low or declining Net Volume, it may signal a fakeout and a possible price pullback. In the case of identifying hidden selling, a trader can use declining Net Volume at price peaks to look for confirming reversal candlestick patterns or divergences on other oscillators.
