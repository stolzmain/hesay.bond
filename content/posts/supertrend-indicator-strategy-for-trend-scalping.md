## Technical Basis of the SuperTrend Indicator

The SuperTrend indicator is rightfully considered one of the best tools for visualizing trends. Its calculation is based on two key components: Average True Range (ATR) and the median price of the asset. ATR measures current volatility, allowing the indicator to adapt to changing market conditions. Unlike standard moving averages, SuperTrend is more sensitive to sharp impulses and holds positions longer during prolonged directional moves. The indicator line changes color and position relative to the price chart depending on whether a set volatility level is breached. When the price closes above the line, the indicator turns green and moves below the chart, signaling bullish sentiment. The opposite situation—a red line moving above the chart—indicates bearish dominance.

## Optimizing Settings for M1-M5

For effective scalping, default SuperTrend settings (10; 3) may prove too slow, resulting in significant signal lag. Professional analysts recommend experimenting with the ATR period and multiplier. For the M1 timeframe, it is optimal to use settings of (10; 2) or even (12; 3) to reduce the number of false signals during sideways movement. A lower multiplier makes the line more sensitive to changes, which allows for capturing short impulses but increases the risk of loss during minor pullbacks. It is important to remember that scalping with this strategy works best on liquid assets with low spreads: major currency pairs, top cryptocurrencies, or index futures. High liquidity guarantees order execution without significant slippage, which could otherwise wipe out the profit from a short-term trade.

## Entry Points and Impulse Logic

The signal to open a position is the moment the SuperTrend indicator changes color. However, in scalping, entering on the first candle after a trend change is not always justified. The most reliable entry point is formed by a combination of two factors: the color change and a retest of the indicator line. If the price returns to the SuperTrend line after an impulsive breakout without closing on the other side, it confirms the strength of the new trend and provides an opportunity to enter with a minimal stop-loss. For Long positions, wait for the line to turn green and the price to fix above it. For Short positions, wait for the line to turn red and the price to remain below it. An important condition is the slope of the line: the steeper it is, the stronger the current impulse. A horizontal SuperTrend often indicates that the market has entered a consolidation phase, where trading based on trend indicators carries increased risk.

## Filtering Market Noise

No indicator is a holy grail, and SuperTrend is no exception. During periods of flat price action, it can produce a series of false signals.
