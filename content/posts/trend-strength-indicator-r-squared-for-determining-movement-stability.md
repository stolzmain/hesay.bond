In essence, R-squared quantifies how closely price data conforms to a linear regression line over a specific period, thereby assessing the strength and predictability of a trend. This oscillator fluctuates between 0 and 1 (or 0% to 100%), with higher values indicating a greater proportion of price variation explained by the linear trend.

## The Essence of the R-squared Indicator

R-squared is a statistical measure that shows the proportion of variance in a dependent variable (price) that is explained by the independent variable (time) in a regression model. If the R-squared is 0.7, it means that 70% of the price action can be explained by a linear relationship, while 30% is attributed to random noise. A high indicator value signals a strong, linear trend with minimal random noise.

## Interpreting Values

High R-squared values, approaching 1, indicate a strong correlation and a well-defined trend where price movements closely follow the regression line. Values above 0.7 often confirm the presence of a clearly defined trend, making trend-following strategies more reliable. Conversely, low values near 0 indicate weak correlation, suggesting that the trend line poorly reflects price action and the market is characterized by noise or sideways movement. Values below 0.3 may signal uncertain or erratic movements. It is important to understand that R-squared evaluates the strength and reliability of a trend, but not its direction.

## Determining Trend Strength

For traders using trend-following strategies, R-squared is an indispensable tool for confirming the sustainability of a move. It allows traders to distinguish a true, strong trend from temporary fluctuations. Consistently high R-squared values confirm a stable trend phase, increasing confidence in chosen strategies. A low R-squared, on the other hand, signals a range or consolidation where movements are less predictable, which may prompt a rethink of tactics or waiting for a clearer trend.

## Practical Application in Trading

The application of R-squared in trading is multifaceted. It can be used to validate trends identified by other indicators. High R-squared values confirm the strength and sustainability of a trend. For swing and position traders, longer timeframes are advisable to obtain stable R-squared values. The indicator also improves risk management: higher R-squared values indicate less randomness, making trend-following strategies more reliable. R-squared helps identify market regimes: when values are high, moving averages can be applied; when they are low, oscillators are preferred.

## Combining with Other Indicators

The effectiveness of R-squared increases significantly when combined with other technical indicators. A common approach is using it with the Linear Regression Slope. R-squared determines the strength of the trend, while the slope determines its direction. Signals are generated in the direction of the slope, provided that R-squared exceeds a specific confidence level (e.g., 95%). Another method involves combining it with oscillators such as the Stochastic Oscillator or RSI. Here, signals are formed based on oscillator readings (overbought/oversold) when R-squared remains low, indicating a less trendy market and a potential reversal. This combination helps identify divergences and assess trend strength. High R-squared values can also signal short-term market predictability.
