## Fundamentals of Volume Profile

Key components of the Volume Profile include several essential elements. The Point of Control (POC) is the price level where the maximum volume was traded during the selected period. The POC often acts as a magnet for price, attracting it or signaling accumulation zones. The Value Area (VA) is the price range where, typically, 68-70% of the total volume was traded. This area is considered the fair value zone, where the majority of market participants agree to trade. The Value Area High (VAH) and Value Area Low (VAL) serve as dynamic support and resistance levels. High Volume Nodes (HVN) are peaks in the profile indicating significant trading activity where the price tends to linger. Conversely, Low Volume Nodes (LVN) are areas with minimal activity that the price often passes through quickly.

## Application in Trading Strategies

The flexible volume profile is indispensable for identifying significant support and resistance levels, as it reveals zones where large market participants have accumulated or distributed positions. When price approaches an HVN or VAH/VAL, it may indicate a potential stall, reversal, or breakout confirmation. For example, a breakout above the VAH with high volume may confirm a bullish trend. Using this tool allows traders to more accurately define entry and exit points, as well as stop-loss and take-profit placements, as these levels are based on actual trading activity.

## Interpreting Profile Shapes

Volume profile shapes can tell a great deal about market sentiment and structure.

A D-shaped profile (or bell-shaped) indicates a balanced market where buyers and sellers are in equilibrium. Most of the volume is concentrated in the middle, and the POC is close to the center.

A P-shaped profile usually forms after a strong price rise followed by consolidation. The wide part is at the top, which is often interpreted as a bullish signal indicating that buyers are in control.

A b-shaped profile is the opposite of a P-shape and occurs after a sharp drop followed by consolidation. The wide part is at the bottom, which is usually considered a bearish signal reflecting selling pressure.

A B-shaped profile (or double distribution) suggests the existence of two high-activity areas separated by a low-volume zone, which may indicate a trend continuation.

## Advantages of the Indicator

Using the flexible volume profile offers several significant advantages. It provides an objective approach to analyzing price levels, reducing the subjectivity of technical analysis. The indicator reveals the market's true intentions by highlighting zones where volume, not just price, confirms an asset's value. It is adaptable to various trading styles—from scalping to position trading—and integrates well with other tools such as Fibonacci levels or moving averages.
