## Mathematical Precision of Fibonacci Levels
The Fibonacci retracement tool is based on the golden ratio and is reflected in the behavior of market cycles and crowd psychology. The key levels—38.2%, 50%, and 61.8%—act as invisible psychological barriers where the price often completes a corrective move before resuming the main trend. The 61.8% level is considered the most significant (the golden ratio) and attracts liquidity from major players. These zones act as dynamic support. When the price pulls back to these values after a strong bullish impulse, the probability of trend continuation increases significantly. However, a mere touch of the level does not guarantee an automatic reversal. Fibonacci levels indicate where a trader should look for a trade, but a specific trigger confirming the market's reaction to this mathematical area is required for a justified entry.

## Synergy of Candlestick Analysis and Levels
The combination of a Hammer and Fibonacci levels creates a powerful effect of confluence, which is highly valued in institutional trading. An ideal setup occurs when the price makes a technical pullback to the 0.5–0.618 zone and forms a Hammer right there. This overlap confirms that the support level is indeed being defended by buyers. If the lower shadow of the candle pierces the Fibonacci level and closes above it, this indicates a false breakout and a hunt for retail stop-losses before the true upward move. Such a signal becomes a compelling argument for opening a long position, as it combines the mathematical precision of market geometry with the clear psychological reaction of the crowd. The risk in such trades is minimal compared to the profit potential, which ensures a high expectancy for the trading strategy.

## Algorithm for Opening and Managing Positions
To effectively work with this methodology, one must adhere to a strict algorithm. First, the dominant bullish trend is determined on a higher timeframe. The Fibonacci tool is drawn from the beginning of the impulse to its peak. The trader waits for a correction into the zone between 50% and 61.8%. Upon the appearance of a Hammer, the position is opened upon the breakout of that candle's high on the following bar. The stop-loss is placed below the pattern's lower shadow with a small buffer to avoid being stopped out by market noise. Take-profit targets are distributed along the extension levels: the first target is the 0 level (the previous local high), and the main target is the 1.618 extension. The color of the candle body is not a deciding factor; however, a bullish (green) Hammer provides an additional statistical advantage, confirming the local victory of buyers in the moment.
