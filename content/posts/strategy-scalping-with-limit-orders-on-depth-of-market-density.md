In this context, density refers to an abnormally large volume of limit orders for buy or sell that acts as a psychological and financial barrier to current price movement.

## Identifying True Densities in the Order Book

## The primary task for a professional scalper is to separate significant volumes from market noise. The concept of density is always relative and depends on the liquidity of a specific financial instrument. For secondary tier assets, density may be considered a few thousand lots, whereas on highly liquid futures or popular crypto pairs, we are talking about tens or hundreds of thousands of contracts. To determine the significance of a volume, a trader analyzes the average turnover over a five-minute period and compares it to the placed order. If the volume in the order book exceeds the average five-minute volume by several times, such a point becomes a potential zone for a bounce or breakout trade.

## Necessary Tools for Rapid Analysis

## Standard terminals are often insufficient for effective implementation of this strategy due to data latency. Professionals use specialized scalping platforms such as Tiger.Trade, EasyScalp, or CScalp. These tools allow for visualizing the order book vertically, displaying not only limit orders but also the Time & Sales (tape) and cluster analysis (Footprint). The most important addition to the order book is the tape: it shows whether market orders are being executed against the density. If the price touches a large volume but the tape slows down, it confirms the strength of the level. If the tape accelerates and the density begins to melt quickly, it is a signal of a potential capitulation of a large player.

## Entry Mechanics and Execution Logic

## The bounce off density strategy involves placing a limit order just in front of a large bid or ask. For example, if there is a significant buy volume in the order book at 150.00, a trader places a buy limit at 150.01. This is done to guarantee execution, as the price may not reach the density level by a single tick. The core logic is that the large volume acts as a shield: the market will require significant effort and time to eat through this wall. Take-profit in scalping is usually secured at the first impulse of the bounce, ranging from 0.1% to 0.5% of the asset price, which provides substantial returns when using leverage.

## Strict Risk Management and Stop-Loss

## The main advantage of scalping on density is the ability to set a maximally short and justified stop-loss. The protective order is placed immediately behind the density (in our example, at 149.99). As soon as the volume in the order book disappears—either as a result of it being consumed by market players or due to its cancellation by the algorithm itself—the rationale for the trade is gone, and the position must be closed immediately. The mathematical expectation in such operations is skewed in favor of the trader: a risk of 2-3 pips is covered by a potential profit of 10-20 pips. Discipline in adhering to stops is a critical factor, as slippage during the consumption of density can be significant.
