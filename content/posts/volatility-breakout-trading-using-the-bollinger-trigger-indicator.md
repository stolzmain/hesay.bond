## Mechanics of the Bollinger Trigger
To precisely capture volatility squeezes, professionals use the Bollinger Band Trigger (BBT). It is based on comparing Bollinger Bands to Keltner Channels or calculating the width of the range. When the outer Bollinger bands move inside the Keltner Channels, it signals a critical drop in volatility. This state is known as a squeeze. On the indicator chart, this period is displayed by a change in the histogram color. For a trader, this is the main signal: the spring is being coiled and is ready to fire in either direction.

## Algorithm for Finding an Entry Point
Trading breakouts requires patience. You wait for the signal while the bands are in a squeezed state. The entry point occurs when the price breaks out of the range as the Bollinger Bands expand. The bands must diverge in opposite directions. A long entry is executed when a candle closes above the upper band and the trigger histogram exits the squeeze to the upside. For a short, the necessary condition is a candle close below the lower band as the trigger shifts into a bearish phase. It is important to wait for the candle to close to avoid fakeouts.

## Signal Filtering and Traps
Market noise often leads to false breakouts, where the price temporarily moves outside the range and then retreats. To minimize risks, filters are used. It is useful to analyze trading volumes: a true breakout is accompanied by increased volume, confirming the interest of major market participants. An additional filter can be the Average Directional Index (ADX), which indicates the birth of a trend, or a long-term moving average to trade strictly in the direction of the global trend.

## Risk Management and Profit Taking
Risk control is of paramount importance. When trading breakouts, the stop-loss is usually placed behind the opposite boundary of the squeeze or below the middle Bollinger line. If the price returns behind the middle line, the scenario is invalidated, and the trade should be closed. It is wise to take profits in parts. The first take-profit is fixed at a 1:2 risk-reward ratio. The remainder of the position should be held using a trailing stop along the middle band until the trend finally exhausts itself.

## Practical Conclusion
Using the Bollinger Trigger allows for systematizing breakout trading, sparing the trader from having to guess the direction of movement before it begins. This methodology requires strict discipline, adherence to money management rules, and an understanding of the market context. Combining the indicator with volume and trend filters helps create a robust trading system that shows stable results across various timeframes and asset classes.
