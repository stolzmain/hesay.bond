## Trading Based on Support and Resistance: The Concept of Flip Levels

Trading based on support and resistance levels is a fundamental method of technical analysis. These levels represent price points where historical data shows a shift in price direction.

## Defining Support and Resistance

A support level is a price zone where a downward trend typically stops and reverses upward. Here, demand outweighs supply. A resistance level is a price zone where an upward trend typically stops and reverses downward. Here, supply outweighs demand. These levels are formed due to market participant psychology and their collective decision-making regarding buying or selling an asset.

## The Importance of Flip Levels

So-called flip levels are of particular importance in trading. These are price points that previously acted in one role (e.g., support) and then, after being broken, began to perform the opposite function (resistance) – and vice versa. Understanding and identifying such levels gives a trader a significant edge.

## Formation Mechanism

When the price breaks through a support level, former buyers who were waiting for a bounce from this level now find themselves at a loss and will look to sell the asset upon a retest to exit their positions with minimal losses. At the same time, sellers who opened short positions during the breakout will defend their profits by adding new sell orders. Thus, former support turns into resistance. A similar process occurs during a resistance breakout: former sellers will buy to cut their losses, and buyers will buy to open new positions, turning resistance into support.

## Identifying Flip Levels

To trade successfully, you must learn to correctly identify flip levels. This can be done by analyzing historical data on the chart. Look for price points where the price bounced repeatedly and then, after a breakout, retested the same mark but with the opposite reaction. The more such confirmations exist across different timeframes, the stronger the flip level is considered. Visually, it looks like a horizontal line that mirrors price movements.

## Trading Strategies

Using flip levels can form the basis for various trading strategies. One popular approach is trading the retest after a breakout. For example, if the price breaks a resistance level and it becomes support, a trader might consider buying upon the first pullback to this level. The stop-loss is placed just below the level, and the take-profit is set at the next significant resistance. It is crucial to wait for confirmation of the price reaction at the level (e.g., the formation of a reversal pattern). Another strategy is trading the bounce from a flip level when the price fails to break through it.

## Confirmation with Indicators

Although flip levels are a powerful tool on their own, their effectiveness can be enhanced by using additional indicators. For example, trading volume can confirm the strength of a breakout or bounce. Increased volume during a breakout indicates validity, while low volume during a retest might suggest a fakeout. Oscillators like RSI or Stochastic can show overbought or oversold conditions when approaching a level, which increases the probability of a reversal.

## The Psychological Aspect

Psychology plays a key role in the formation and function of flip levels. The collective expectations and actions of traders create self-fulfilling prophecies. Understanding this aspect helps a trader maintain composure and make rational decisions rather than succumbing to emotions.
