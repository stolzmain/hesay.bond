## Calculation Mechanics and Key Parameters

The mathematical model of Absolute Trend Strength is based on modified Average True Range (ATR) and Relative Strength Index (RSI) algorithms, integrated into a unified volatility assessment system. The main difference of ATS lies in separating market pressure into two independent components: the strength of the bullish flow and the strength of the bearish pressure. The algorithm compares current price changes against historical values over a specific period, normalizing the data so that the trader can view the relative power of a move on a scale from 0 to 100. An important configuration parameter is the smoothing period, which allows the indicator to be adapted to different timeframes, ranging from scalping on minute charts to long-term investing. This flexibility minimizes the impact of random price spikes while maintaining sensitivity to emerging structural market changes.

## Visual Analysis of Market Dynamics

On the chart, Absolute Trend Strength is typically represented as two lines of different colors, often complemented by a histogram or signal levels. The first line displays buyer strength, and the second displays seller strength. When the bull power line is significantly above the bear line and trending upward, it signals a sustainable uptrend. The intersection of these lines is a classic signal of a shift in market dominance. Professionals pay special attention to the position of the lines relative to critical levels (e.g., 20 and 80). If both lines are in the lower part of the range, it indicates a consolidation phase or flat market, where opening positions is extremely risky. A breakout of one of the lines from the stagnation zone is interpreted as an impulsive move, which often marks the beginning of a powerful trend supported by institutional capital.

## Entry and Exit Signals

The practical application of ATS comes down to identifying zones of extreme dominance by one side. The primary trading signal is considered to be the intersection of the bull and bear lines combined with the overall trend direction on a higher timeframe. If the bull line crosses the bear line from bottom to top, it forms a buy signal. However, the highest quality entry points emerge during moments of divergence: when price hits a new high, but the absolute trend strength reading begins to decline. This is a clear sign of exhaustion in momentum and a likely reversal or deep correction. Another effective method is using the zero point or central axis: if the strength of the dominant side begins to drop below this level, it serves as a signal to take profit, as the probability of continued directional movement drops sharply.
