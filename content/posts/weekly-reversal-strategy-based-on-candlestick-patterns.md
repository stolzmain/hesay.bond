The Weekly Reversal strategy is rooted in the principle of market cycle completion within the five-day trading week. ## Key Patterns for Identifying Reversals

The strategy is based on three fundamental candlestick models that have proven their efficiency over decades. The first is the Pin Bar. This is a candle with a small body and an abnormally long wick pointing against the current trend. A long tail indicates that the price reached a zone of critical resistance and was aggressively rejected. The second model is the Engulfing pattern. This occurs when the body of the current candle completely covers the range of the previous one. This is a direct signal of a sharp shift in market control. The third model is the Morning or Evening Star. These are three-candle formations where the central element represents market indecision, and the final candle confirms a trend reversal. Each of these models on a weekly chart serves as a powerful trigger, indicating that the current move is exhausted.

## Filtering Signals and Liquidity Levels

A candlestick pattern on its own is only half the battle. For the strategy to produce stable results, the model must be anchored to a strong horizontal support or resistance level. The ideal scenario is a false breakout of a significant high or low from the previous month or quarter, ending with the formation of a reversal candle. An additional filter can be divergence on oscillators, such as RSI or MACD. If the price updates a local high while the indicator shows a lower peak, it confirms a loss of momentum. It is important to understand: we are not just looking for a pretty geometric shape, but the moment when the market has exhausted its potential and accumulated energy for a correction or a full-scale reversal. Confluence between a candlestick pattern and an important Fibonacci level also exponentially increases the probability of a successful trade.

## Entry Points and Risk Management

Entry into a position is executed at the start of a new trading week once the previous candle has fully formed. There are two methods: aggressive and conservative. The aggressive approach implies opening a market order immediately after the terminal opens on Monday. The conservative approach requires placing a limit order at the 50% retracement level of the signal candle's body. This significantly reduces the size of the stop-loss and increases the mathematical expectation of profit, although there is a risk that the price will head to the target without a correction. A stop-loss is always placed behind the extreme point of the reversal pattern with a small buffer. This is the zone where a breakout invalidates the entire trade thesis. Strictly following these rules eliminates emotional errors and turns trading into a structured process.

## Position Management and Profit Taking

Since the work is done on a weekly chart, targets must correspond to the scale of the move. The risk/reward ratio in this strategy is rarely less than 1:3, which allows for consistent profitability even with a 40% win rate.
