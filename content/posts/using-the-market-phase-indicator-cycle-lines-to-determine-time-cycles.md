## The essence of time cycles
Market cycles are repeating patterns of asset price growth and decline driven by economic conditions, investor sentiment, and external events. They are not limited to economics alone but also reflect the psychological reactions of market participants to price changes. There are four main phases: accumulation, markup, distribution, and markdown. These phases form a complex structure, as short-term cycles can be nested within longer ones. For instance, short-term corrections can occur within a long-term bull market. Identifying the current phase and knowing statistically favorable periods for trading (such as bullish or bearish months/days) can significantly increase efficiency.

## The Cycle Lines indicator
The Cycle Lines indicator is a tool for identifying recurring, time-based cyclical patterns in financial markets. These lines appear on a price chart as vertical markers placed at equal intervals and are designed to highlight potential market trends and cyclical pivot points. Unlike traditional price-based indicators, Cycle Lines focus exclusively on the temporal aspect rather than price action. They help visually segment the chart by time, which is not always readily available to the user by default.

## Application in trading
Using Cycle Lines allows traders to systematize their approach to market timing. First, they help identify cyclical patterns by analyzing price behavior near these vertical markers to detect recurring trends. Second, Cycle Lines are used to precisely pinpoint entry and exit points, aligning trades with established cyclical pivot points. This can be especially useful for assets with predictable price rhythms, such as stocks with seasonal trends or commodities. For systematic traders, Cycle Lines serve as a tool for defining exact entry and exit points, reducing the risk of impulse trades. They can be used as "time stops" to track the exit moment from a trade after a specific number of days.

## Combining with other tools
The effectiveness of Cycle Lines increases significantly when integrated with other technical analysis tools. A reliable systematic strategy involving Cycle Lines should not rely solely on time signals but must also include price-based confirmations to improve accuracy. For example, Cycle Lines can be integrated into trend-following strategies, where they help determine entry points after a pullback within an existing trend. Traders can refine their entries by confirming price action near a Cycle Line using moving averages or momentum indicators. In mean reversion strategies, Cycle Lines may coincide with price exhaustion points and, when combined with oscillators like RSI or Bollinger Bands, help identify short-term reversals.
