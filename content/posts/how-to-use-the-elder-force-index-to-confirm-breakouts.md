## Calculation and interpretation of the EFI
The Elder Force Index is calculated based on the current close, the previous close, and trading volume. The formula for a one-period EFI is (Current Close - Previous Close)  Volume. Moving averages of the EFI, such as a 13-period EMA, are often used for smoothing. Positive EFI values indicate bull dominance, while negative ones indicate bear dominance. An increase in volume during strong price movement leads to sharp spikes in the EFI, which is a sign of strong momentum. The zero line of the EFI serves as a watershed between bullish and bearish sentiment. Divergences between the EFI and price often signal potential reversals.

## EFI for breakout confirmation
Using the Elder Force Index to confirm breakouts is one of the most effective strategies. A breakout of a major resistance or support level is considered reliable if it is accompanied by a corresponding spike in the EFI. In a bullish breakout (breaking resistance), we look for a significant increase in the positive EFI, indicating that the move is reinforced by strong buying pressure and volume. Conversely, in a bearish breakout (breaking support), a significant drop in the negative EFI is expected, indicating strong selling pressure and high volume. The absence of such confirmation may point to a fakeout, allowing traders to avoid losses.

## Example of a bullish breakout
Imagine an asset trading in a range while approaching long-term resistance. When the price breaks above it, watch the EFI. If the EFI surges above zero at the moment of the breakout and shows significant growth, it is strong confirmation that the breakout is genuine. High positive volume and rising prices testify to an influx of major buyers. This provides a rationale for entering a long position with increased confidence, as the breakout is backed by real market force. Ignoring such confirmation or its absence increases the risk of falling into a fakeout trap.

## Example of a bearish breakout
Similarly, an asset in an uptrend consolidates as it approaches key support. If the price breaks below this level, we pay attention to the EFI. If the indicator drops sharply below zero and shows a significant negative value, it confirms the strength of the bearish breakout. High negative volume and declining prices indicate aggressive selling and the exit of major players. Such confirmation from the EFI signals an entry for a short position, as the downward breakout is supported by significant selling pressure, reducing the likelihood of a quick bounce.

## Combining with other tools
Although the Elder Force Index is powerful, its effectiveness multiplies when used with other indicators and analysis methods. Breakout confirmation via EFI becomes more convincing if it occurs against the backdrop of moving average crossovers, the formation of technical analysis patterns, or when the price reaches key Fibonacci levels. Using the EFI in conjunction with volatility indicators, such as ATR, can also provide additional context.
