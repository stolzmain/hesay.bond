 is the Adam and Eve. This formation is a complex variation of the classic Double Bottom or Double Top, but with a unique asymmetry that provides more accurate signals regarding the intentions of smart money. First detailed by renowned expert Thomas Bulkowski, this pattern reflects the transition from panic selling to systematic asset accumulation, making it a powerful tool in a professional analyst's arsenal.

## Anatomy and Psychology of the Formation

## The essence of the pattern lies in the formation of two successive lows that differ in structure. The first low, Adam, is characterized by a sharp, V-shaped drop and an equally rapid rebound against a spike in trading volume. This is a classic selling climax accompanied by high volatility. The second low, Eve, forms significantly more slowly. It is a rounded, U-shaped consolidation where the price lingers at low levels without retesting the Adam extreme, or perhaps making only a minor breach before a quick recovery. Psychologically, Adam represents fear and panic, while Eve represents stabilization and the gradual absorption of liquidity by institutional investors.

## Identification Rules on the Chart

## For a pattern to be considered valid, several conditions must be met. First, Adam must be visually sharper and deeper than Eve. Second, there must be a local peak between the two lows, the level of which becomes the critical neckline. It is important to note that the lows do not have to be at the same level—Eve can be higher or slightly lower than Adam, but the roundness of the second base is a mandatory attribute. If the second trough also appears sharp, you are looking at a standard Double Bottom, which follows different statistical patterns.

## Entry Technique

## The trading strategy for this pattern involves entering the market the moment the reversal is confirmed. The key signal is the breakout of the neckline—a resistance level drawn through the intermediate peak. Traders use two approaches to open a trade. The aggressive method involves entering with a market order immediately after a candle closes above the resistance level. The conservative method requires waiting for a retest of the broken level from the top down. The retest often provides the best risk-to-reward ratio, as the former resistance zone becomes support, confirming buyer strength.

## Risk Management and Targets

## Proper risk management is the foundation of trading survival. When trading the Adam and Eve pattern, the stop-loss is traditionally placed below the lowest point of the rounded Eve base. This is logical, as breaking this low completely invalidates the bullish scenario. The target level (take-profit) is calculated mathematically: measure the height of the pattern (the distance from the deepest low to the neckline) and project it upward from the breakout point. Experience shows that the price often covers a distance equal to 100% of the formation's height, though taking partial profit at the 60–70% level helps secure the position.

## The Role of Volume and Indicators

## To increase the probability of a successful pattern outcome, confirming filters must be used. Trading volume should increase significantly during the formation of Adam and drop noticeably during the formation of Eve. An explosive surge in volume at the moment of the neckline breakout serves as the final confirmation of the move's validity. Additionally, it is recommended to use oscillators such as RSI or MACD.
