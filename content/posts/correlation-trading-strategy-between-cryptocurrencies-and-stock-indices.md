## Bitcoin in the Mirror of Stock Indices

Historically, Bitcoin was positioned as digital gold, a safe-haven asset independent of the traditional banking system. However, since 2020, following the massive influx of institutional investors and the launch of ETFs, the situation has changed drastically. Today, the correlation coefficient between BTC and the tech-heavy Nasdaq index often exceeds 0.8, indicating near-synchronous movement. This happens because large funds manage their portfolios using algorithms that automatically buy or sell risk assets based on the macroeconomic backdrop. If the US Federal Reserve softens its rhetoric, stock indices surge, dragging the crypto market along with them. At such moments, a trader seeing a breakout of a local high on the S&P 500 can, with a high degree of probability, expect a similar move in Bitcoin within minutes or hours.

## Intermarket Analysis Toolkit

To implement this strategy, you must monitor several charts simultaneously. First is the US Dollar Index (DXY), which maintains a stable inverse correlation with risk assets. When the dollar weakens, stock indices and cryptocurrencies rise. Second, analyze S&P 500 and Nasdaq 100 futures. Special attention should be paid to the pre-market period and the US market open. This is when the day's primary trends are established. Professionals use specialized terminals or the TradingView Compare tool to overlay charts. If the Nasdaq has already printed a new daily high while Bitcoin is still chopping in a range, a price inefficiency emerges, which can be exploited for entry.

## Tactics for Front-Running Trades

Correlation trading requires high focus and rapid decision-making. The core of the method is hunting for divergences. For instance, if the S&P 500 begins a sharp decline while a cryptocurrency holds its support levels, it signals hidden buying strength in the crypto sector. Conversely, if the stock market is rising but BTC is stagnant, it is a reason for caution, as a sudden catch-up impulse is possible. The trader places stop-losses behind the nearest liquidity levels on the crypto side, while taking cues from the lead indices. It is important to remember that correlation is not always 100 percent. There are periods of decoupling, where industry-specific news, such as exchange hacks or regulatory changes, becomes the dominant factor, temporarily breaking the link with the traditional market.

## The Dangers of Market Divergence and Decoupling

Despite its high reliability, this strategy has its pitfalls. A trader must clearly distinguish between short-term noise and a sustainable trend.
