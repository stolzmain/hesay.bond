## Participant Psychology Before News
Prior to the release of significant macroeconomic data, such as Federal Reserve interest rate decisions or inflation reports, uncertainty dominates the market. Traders split into two camps: those who close positions to avoid risk, and those who place pending orders outside the squeeze range. Declining trading volumes within the pattern indicate that liquidity is migrating to the outer boundaries of the consolidation. The psychological aspect lies in the anticipation of a strong move. When the news hits, the triggering of initial stop-losses and entry orders creates a chain reaction, turning the narrow corridor into a launchpad for an aggressive rally or a dump. The market instantly shifts from a state of equilibrium to a directional trend phase.

## Technical Identification of the Pattern
To identify a squeeze, professional analysts use a combination of chart analysis and indicators. One of the most reliable tools is the Bollinger Band Squeeze. When the indicator lines narrow to a historical low, it signals a critical drop in volatility. The Average True Range (ATR) indicator provides additional confirmation, with values that should sit at local lows during the pattern formation. Monitoring volume is essential: it should gradually decline as the range tightens. If symmetrical structures form on the chart during this process, it indicates a parity between bulls and bears that will be broken immediately after the news drops.

## Impact of Fundamental Catalysts
Price squeezes rarely happen without cause. This is a standby mode typical for key events on the economic calendar. The most powerful moves following a Price Squeeze are observed during U.S. Non-Farm Payrolls and Consumer Price Index (CPI) releases. The unique feature of this pre-news pattern is that the market ignores minor signals, focusing exclusively on the upcoming event. During this period, technical analysis becomes secondary to the expected impulse; however, the boundaries of the squeeze determine the exact price levels where mass execution of defensive and breakout orders will begin. The news here acts merely as a mechanism that unlocks accumulated volume.

## Breakout Strategy
Trading this pattern requires patience and refraining from guessing the direction. There are two primary tactics. The first is placing a pair of pending orders (Buy Stop and Sell Stop) just outside the squeeze range. This allows you to capture the impulse regardless of direction. The second, more conservative tactic involves entering after the initial impulse followed by a retest of the broken range boundary. This protects the trader from fakeouts and whipsaws—sharp moves in both directions that often occur in the first seconds of a data release.
