## The Nature of Market Basket Formation

## Pattern formation begins with a protracted downtrend that gradually slows down and transitions into a sideways move. Visually, the basket is a wide, rounded bottom where the asset price consolidates in a narrow range. At this stage, supply is absorbed from weak hands—retail investors frustrated by the lack of price action who exit the asset. Large capital, conversely, uses this period to gradually build a position without causing sharp price spikes. The bottom of the basket should be smooth; sudden V-shaped bounces can indicate instability and undermine the logic of accumulation. The longer an asset stays in the bottoming phase, the more significant the subsequent price move will be, as the accumulated liquidity will require a powerful impulse to manifest.

## Pattern Geometry and Proportions

## To classify the formation as a Basket Handle, it is essential to observe specific price proportions. After the asset price recovers to the local resistance level established at the beginning of the decline, the handle phase occurs. This is a short-term correction or sideways consolidation that should not exceed 30% of the basket's depth. If the pullback is too deep, it indicates persistent selling pressure and casts doubt on buyer strength. An optimal handle is slightly downward sloping or takes the form of a flag. It is crucial that the handle forms in the upper third of the overall structure. This demonstrates the market's readiness for a breakout, as sellers can no longer significantly push the price below the established resistance levels, which are now turning into support.

## The Importance of Volume in Trend Confirmation

## Trading volume serves as the primary filter for verifying the pattern. On the left side of the basket, during the price drop, volume may be elevated, reflecting panic. In the center of the bottom, trading activity should drop significantly, reaching its lows—a sign that supply has dried up. The key moment arrives during the handle formation phase: volume here should be extremely low, indicating a lack of desire among market participants to sell the asset at current levels. However, at the moment of the breakout above the handle's upper boundary (resistance line), volume must surge, at least 1.5–2 times higher than the 20-period average. This spike in liquidity confirms the genuine intention of institutional investors to trigger an upward distribution phase.

## Trading Entry Mechanics and Confirmation

## The entry point is defined at the moment the price crosses the upper boundary of the handle. Professional traders prefer to wait for the candle to close above the resistance level on the active timeframe to minimize the risk of a fakeout. There are two primary methods regarding entry aggressiveness: entering on the breakout itself or on the retest of the broken level, which now acts as support. The latter is considered safer but carries the risk of missing the trade if the impulse proves too strong.
