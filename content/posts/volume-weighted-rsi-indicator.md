To solve this, the Volume-Weighted RSI (VW-RSI) was developed, integrating liquidity metrics directly into the oscillator to create a deeper and more accurate picture of market sentiment.

## The Evolution of a Classic Oscillator

The traditional RSI measures the speed and change of price movements, operating in a range from 0 to 100. But price is only one dimension of the market. The second, equally important one, is volume. It is volume that confirms the validity of a move. If the price rises on low volume, it often indicates a weakness in bulls and a potential reversal. The VW-RSI combines these two metrics, assigning greater weight to price fluctuations accompanied by significant capital inflows. Thus, the indicator becomes less sensitive to market noise occurring during periods of low liquidity and responds more promptly to the actions of whales.

## Mechanics of Volume Weighting

The mathematical foundation of VW-RSI is based on a modification of Welles Wilder's standard formula. Instead of simply averaging positive and negative closes over a specific period (usually 14 candles), the algorithm multiplies each price change by the corresponding trading volume. As a result, the weighted relative strength reflects not just quote dynamics, but real cash flow. When a powerful impulse occurs with volume confirmation, the VW-RSI moves much more actively than its classic counterpart. If the price moves by inertia with an empty order book, the indicator remains neutral, warning the trader against entering a questionable trade.

## The Advantage of the Weighting Factor

The main advantage of the Volume-Weighted RSI lies in its ability to filter out fakeouts. Financial markets often see situations where the price briefly breaches resistance or support levels on low volume—so-called liquidity traps. A standard RSI may show an exit into the overbought zone at such moments, triggering a premature sell. Thanks to the weighting factor, the VW-RSI smooths out such spikes, showing that there is no real strength behind the move. This makes it an indispensable tool for positional trading and navigating volatile assets, such as cryptocurrencies or tech sector stocks.

## Practical Trading Signals

Divergences and working with extreme zones remain the primary signals for the VW-RSI. However, their quality is significantly higher. A bullish divergence on the VW-RSI, where the price hits a new low while the indicator forms a higher trough on rising volume, is considered one of the most reliable reversal signals. Regarding the 30 and 70 levels, flexibility is advised. Unlike the standard RSI, the volume-weighted version can stay in overbought zones longer during a strong trend backed by liquidity. This allows a trader to hold a profitable position longer without closing at the first sign of technical overheating.

## Combining with Other Tools

To achieve maximum efficiency, professional analysts recommend using the VW-RSI in conjunction with trend indicators, such as moving averages or Bollinger Bands. The indicator excels in its role as a confirmation oscillator.
