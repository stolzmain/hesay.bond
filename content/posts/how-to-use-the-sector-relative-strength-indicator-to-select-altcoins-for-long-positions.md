## The Essence of Relative Strength
Relative Strength is a method that evaluates the performance of a sector or coin against Bitcoin (BTC) or the TOTAL3 capitalization index. This method is not related to the RSI technical indicator. Its goal is to find market segments that demonstrate resilience during drawdowns and outperforming growth during recoveries. When Bitcoin consolidates, capital within the altcoin market is redistributed. A sector that maintains support levels or marks local highs during this period demonstrates strength and becomes a priority for trading.

## Identifying a Strong Sector
For practical analysis, traders use comparative charts. On TradingView, this is implemented by dividing a specific sector index by the price of BTC (e.g., DeFi/BTC). An uptrend on such a synthetic chart indicates that the sector is outperforming the flagship cryptocurrency and attracting more attention from buyers. Additionally, screeners tracking percentage price changes over various timeframes are used. A sector that consistently leads across multiple timeframes and shows the shallowest drawdown during market corrections is considered the most promising for long setups.

## Selecting Strong Coins
After identifying the favorite sector, the next step is to choose specific coins for long positions. Within a strong group, assets do not perform identically. The first step involves comparing charts of leading tokens against the sector index or each other (e.g., SOL/ETH). The most promising candidates are the beta leaders—coins that are the first to react to a market reversal, show high trading volume, and break their highs faster than the rest. If a sector has risen by 5% and a token within it by 12% on rising volume, it signals interest from smart money. It is better to ignore weak coins within the same sector.

## Entry Rules and Risks
Entering a long position in a strong altcoin is based on classic technical analysis. The optimal entry point forms during a local price pullback to key support zones (e.g., EMA 20/50 or flip levels) while the asset's relative strength continues to grow. Stop-losses are placed below the nearest local swing low. Relative strength is a dynamic indicator. As soon as the altcoin/BTC ratio chart begins to show weakness or breaks its uptrend line, it serves as a signal to take profit.

Understanding liquidity concentration allows traders to avoid working with weak assets. Using sector relative strength does not guarantee profit in every trade, but it helps a trader act systematically and deliberately, selecting the most resilient altcoins with high growth potential for long positions.
