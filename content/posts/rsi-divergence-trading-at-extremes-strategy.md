When the price hits a new extreme, but the oscillator fails to confirm the move, it signals that the dominant trend is weakening. The logic is simple: price is moving on momentum, but the fuel in the form of market impulse is running dry. Trading divergences at extreme values allows for entries at the very beginning of a correction or a full-scale reversal, providing an advantageous risk-to-reward ratio.

## Configuring the RSI Indicator

To implement this strategy, standard RSI settings (period 14) work for most timeframes, from hourly to daily. However, the key factor is managing overbought and oversold zones. In the classic setup, these are levels 70 and 30. To increase signal precision, experts often use tighter zones—80 and 20. This filters out market noise and focuses only on moments when the price has reached a true emotional peak. It is important to remember that on lower timeframes (M5-M15), the indicator frequently generates fakeouts, which is why intervals of H1 and higher remain the priority for hunting high-quality divergences.

## Identifying Signals at Extremes

A bearish divergence forms when the price chart prints a new Higher High, while the RSI curve prints a Lower High. This occurs in the zone above 70. Such a signal suggests that buyers are losing control, despite the visual price appreciation. A bullish divergence is the mirror image: the price drops to a new Lower Low, but the RSI prints a Higher Low in the zone below 30. This is a clear sign that selling pressure is exhausted. Signals are considered strongest when both peaks of the indicator are located within the extreme zones, highlighting critical market overextension.

## Entry Confirmation Algorithm

Opening a trade blindly right after a discrepancy appears is a classic beginner mistake. A divergence can persist for a long time while the price continues to trend. A professional entry requires a trigger. The optimal approach is to combine the divergence with candlestick patterns. For instance, the appearance of a pin bar, engulfing pattern, or shooting star at the second peak of the divergence significantly strengthens the signal. An additional filter is a trendline breakout on the RSI indicator itself or the curve exiting the extreme zone back into neutral territory. Only after the momentum is graphically confirmed is the order opened.

## Risk Management and Targets

Trading against the current trend always carries increased risk, so strict risk management is mandatory. The stop loss is placed behind the most recent local price extreme with a buffer of 5-10 pips to account for market spreads. Take profit targets are managed in stages. The first target is the nearest significant support or resistance level, where 50% of the position is closed. The remainder is moved to breakeven and held until the RSI reaches the opposite zone or forms a reversal signal. This approach minimizes losses during false reversals and maximizes profit in the event of a global trend shift.

## Psychology and Common Pitfalls

The main trap of this strategy is trading during strong fundamental events. During the release of major economic news, the RSI can stay in the overbought zone for hours, printing multiple divergences while the price continues to pump.
