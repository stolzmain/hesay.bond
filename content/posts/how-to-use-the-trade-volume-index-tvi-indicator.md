ade Volume Index (TVI). This indicator is designed to evaluate the balance of power between buyers and sellers, helping to determine whether an asset is currently undergoing accumulation or distribution. Unlike standard volume metrics, TVI provides a more granular view by processing intraday price changes at the tick level.

## How the indicator works
## The TVI algorithm is based on comparing the current closing price with the previous value on a tick basis or short timeframes. If the price rises and exceeds a specified minimum threshold, the entire volume of the current period is classified as buying volume and added to the indicator total. If the price drops below the threshold, the volume is considered selling volume and is subtracted from the total. If the price change is negligible, the volume direction remains the same as the previous step. This allows for a more accurate assessment of current pressure from market participants.

## Key configuration settings
## The main parameter when configuring TVI is the minimum tick value. This metric determines the indicator sensitivity to market noise. A value that is too low can lead to false signals due to random price fluctuations, while a value that is too high will cause the indicator to lag. Tuning requires adaptation to a specific asset and its current volatility. In terms of behavior, TVI resembles the On-Balance Volume (OBV) indicator; however, TVI is focused on intraday dynamics, making it more adaptable for short-term strategies.

## Signals and practical application
## Traders use TVI to solve several tasks:
 Trend confirmation. If the price is rising and the TVI is trending upward, it indicates capital inflows and buyer strength. A downward direction during a price decline confirms a downtrend.
 Zero line crossovers. A breakout above the zero line signals a potential shift in initiative to buyers, while a breakdown below signals a shift to sellers.
 Identifying divergences. This is one of the key signals. If the price sets a new local high while the TVI forms a lower peak, it indicates weakening buying pressure and a potential market reversal to the downside.

## Nuances and limitations
## Despite its utility, TVI has specific characteristics that must be considered. During periods of deep sideways movement or low volatility, the indicator can produce many false signals while oscillating around the zero line. It is not a standalone trading system. To improve analysis quality, it is advisable to combine it with trend filters, such as moving averages, or with oscillators to gauge overbought and oversold zones.

## A professional approach to the Trade Volume Index requires understanding that volumes only reflect participant activity and do not guarantee a specific trade outcome. A sensible combination of TVI with chart analysis and strict risk management allows for a better assessment of market structure and helps identify moments when large players begin to build their positions.
