## The essence of the Railway Tracks pattern
The Railway Tracks pattern consists of two adjacent candles that have roughly equal body lengths but opposite directions. It signals a potential reversal of the current trend. The first candle reflects the current price direction, while the second indicates a possible shift in sentiment.

## How to spot Railway Tracks on a chart
Visually, the Railway Tracks pattern stands out on a chart because both candles should be long relative to the preceding ones. Key characteristics for identification:

 Two candles of different colors: One candle is bullish (green/white), the other is bearish (red/black).
 Equal body length: The bodies of the candles should be roughly equal in size and at approximately the same level. A slight difference of a few pips is acceptable, but the more identical the candles, the stronger the signal.
 Short wicks: The ideal pattern has short wicks or no wicks at all, which indicates strong buying or selling pressure. If a wick exceeds the body by more than 30 percent, the signal is considered weak.
 Appearance on a trend: The Railway Tracks pattern is most significant when it forms after a sustained trending move.

## Bullish Railway Tracks: Reversal to the upside
A bullish Railway Tracks formation appears during a downtrend. It consists of an initial bearish (falling) candle followed by a bullish (rising) candle of roughly the same size. This indicates that sellers are losing initiative and buyers are starting to take control of the market, which can lead to a price reversal to the upside.

## Bearish Railway Tracks: Reversal to the downside
A bearish Railway Tracks pattern forms during an uptrend. In this case, the first candle is bullish and the second is bearish, both of comparable size. This pattern signals that bullish sentiment is exhausted and sellers are seizing the initiative, foreshadowing a potential price decline.

## Interpreting market sentiment
The Railway Tracks pattern is a prime example of rapid sentiment shifts in the market. It demonstrates that after the dominance of one group of participants (buyers or sellers), opponents enter the market, balancing or even completely reversing the current direction. The equality of the candles in the pattern indicates a balance of power between bulls and bears, which often precedes a reversal.

## Trading strategies with Railway Tracks
Trading the Railway Tracks pattern requires confirmation and adherence to risk management rules:

 Confirmation: Always wait for the full close of the second candle in the pattern. Additional confirmations include increased trading volume on the second candle or the appearance of the pattern near significant support/resistance levels.
 Entry point: For a bullish pattern, a buy order can be placed slightly above the high of the second candle. For a bearish pattern, a sell order can be placed slightly below the low of the second candle.
 Setting a stop-loss: The stop-loss should be placed behind the extreme of the first candle of the pattern (below the low for a bullish setup and above the high for a bearish one). This allows you to manage risk and protect the position from random stop-outs.
 Take-profit: Profit targets can be determined by the nearest significant support/resistance levels. It is recommended to set a take-profit at 1.5 times the stop-loss.

## Limitations and risks
Although Railway Tracks is an informative reversal model, it is not categorized as one of the strongest signals indicating a global trend change.
