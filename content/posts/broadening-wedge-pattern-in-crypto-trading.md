## Anatomy and Visual Characteristics

Visually, the pattern is formed by two diverging trendlines. The upper line connects local peaks, each of which is higher than the previous one, while the lower line connects troughs, each dropping below the preceding one. To confirm the validity of the figure in technical analysis, at least five clear touchpoints are required: three on one side and two on the other. In crypto trading, a broadening wedge is often confused with a standard wide sideways range, but the key difference lies in the angle of the boundary slopes. The steeper the lines diverge, the higher the emotional tension and the more powerful the subsequent impulse will be. It is important to note that the pattern can be slanted (ascending or descending) or horizontal, which directly affects the probability of the breakout direction.

## The Psychology of Market Chaos

The formation of a Megaphone is a direct reflection of a fierce battle between aggressive buyers and equally determined sellers. In a growing crypto market, every new high inspires false optimism and triggers FOMO, leading inexperienced traders to open long positions right at the peak. However, the subsequent sharp drop to a new low causes panic and mass stop-loss triggering. In the crypto sphere, this pattern often foreshadows a global trend reversal or the start of a deep correction. Liquidity is washed out in both directions, the market becomes thin, and any significant fundamental event can trigger a cascading move that finally breaks the structure.

## Entry and Exit Strategies

Trading a broadening wedge requires iron discipline and pinpoint accuracy. There are two main tactics. The first is trading the channel on the rebound. A trader opens a position upon a confirmed touch of the lower boundary, targeting the opposite line. This is risky due to constantly rising volatility, but it offers an extremely favorable risk-to-reward ratio. The second tactic, which is more conservative, is breakout trading. In cryptocurrencies, the price most often breaks downward after the formation of the fifth or sixth touchpoint. A safe entry is executed on the retest of the broken boundary. The target for the move is usually the distance equal to the height of the wedge's widest part, projected from the breakout point. It is important to take profits in parts, as volatility can persist.

## Choosing the Timeframe for Analysis

The pattern performs best on higher timeframes, ranging from the four-hour (4H) to the daily (1D) chart. On lower time intervals, such as 5 or 15 minutes, a broadening wedge often turns out to be mere market noise caused by trading algorithms. On Bitcoin or Ethereum charts, a Megaphone formation on the daily timeframe can take several weeks to materialize, giving the trader enough time to prepare a trading plan. The longer the figure takes to form, the more significant the final price breakout will be.
